# Meridian Technologies — New Hire Onboarding Guide

Welcome to Meridian Technologies! This guide walks you through your first 90 days.

## Before Your First Day

### Account Setup

Your IT welcome email arrives 3 business days before your start date. It contains:
- Google Workspace login (firstname.lastname@meridiantech.com)
- Slack workspace invitation
- Workday HR portal credentials
- VPN setup instructions (for remote access to internal tools)

Please complete the following before Day 1:
1. Set up your Google Workspace account and enable 2-factor authentication
2. Join the Slack workspace and add a profile photo
3. Complete tax and direct deposit forms in Workday
4. Review and sign the Employee Handbook acknowledgment in DocuSign

### Equipment

If working on-site, your laptop and peripherals will be at your desk on Day 1. Remote employees receive a FedEx shipment 2–3 days before their start date containing:
- MacBook Pro 14" (Engineering) or MacBook Air 15" (all other departments)
- External monitor (Dell 27" 4K)
- Keyboard, mouse, and USB-C hub
- Company-branded welcome kit

## Week 1: Orientation

### Day 1

- **9:00 AM**: Welcome session with your manager (30 min)
- **9:30 AM**: HR orientation — benefits enrollment, company policies, office tour (2 hours)
- **11:30 AM**: Team lunch (company-paid)
- **1:00 PM**: IT setup session — install required software, configure VPN and security tools (1 hour)
- **2:00 PM**: Meet your onboarding buddy — a tenured team member assigned to help you get settled
- **3:00 PM**: Begin reviewing team documentation and project wiki

### Days 2–5

- Shadow your onboarding buddy on daily standups and meetings
- Complete mandatory compliance training in the Learning Portal (approximately 3 hours total):
  - Security Awareness Training
  - Anti-Harassment & Inclusion Training
  - Data Privacy Fundamentals
  - Code of Conduct Review
- Set up your development environment (Engineering) or tool access (other departments)
- Schedule introductory 1:1s with key stakeholders your manager identifies

## Week 2–4: Ramp-Up

### Learning the Product

- Complete the "MAP Fundamentals" self-paced course in the Learning Portal (4 hours)
- Attend the weekly Product Demo session (Thursdays at 2:00 PM)
- Create your first test dashboard using the demo dataset
- Review the product roadmap document shared in the #product-updates Slack channel

### Your First Project

Your manager will assign a starter project by the end of Week 2. Starter projects are scoped to be completable within 2–3 weeks and are designed to:
- Familiarize you with the codebase or tooling
- Introduce you to the team's workflow and review process
- Give you a tangible early win

### Key Meetings to Attend

- **Daily standup**: 9:30 AM, 15 minutes (your team's Slack huddle or Zoom)
- **Weekly team sync**: Varies by department, typically 1 hour
- **All-hands meeting**: First Monday of each month, 11:00 AM, in-person or Zoom
- **1:1 with manager**: Weekly, 30 minutes (you set the agenda)

## Days 30–60: Integration

### 30-Day Check-In

Your manager will conduct a formal 30-day check-in to discuss:
- How you're adjusting to the role and team
- Progress on your starter project
- Any blockers, questions, or resource needs
- Initial goals for the next 30 days

### Expanding Your Scope

By Day 30 you should be:
- Attending and contributing to team discussions
- Picking up tasks independently from the sprint board
- Familiar with the team's code review or approval workflows
- Connected with colleagues across at least 2 other teams

## Days 60–90: Full Contribution

### 60-Day Check-In

A brief check-in to ensure you're on track. Topics include:
- Contributions to date and areas of strength
- Any skill gaps to address with training or mentorship
- Feedback on the onboarding experience (we use this to improve the process)

### 90-Day Review

Your manager conducts a formal 90-day review marking the end of your probationary period. This review covers:
- Overall performance assessment
- Confirmation of role expectations and success metrics
- Goal-setting for the next review cycle (mid-year or end-of-year)
- Eligibility for remote work arrangements (if not already granted)

Successful completion of the 90-day review transitions you to regular employee status with full benefits eligibility including 401(k) enrollment and remote work privileges.
